## 2-3 Minute Walkthrough Script

### Intro

Andy, Thurr here.

I put this together so you can see the direction before we get on a call. This is not me trying to sell you random software. This is me showing you how I would clean up the lead path if I was helping you build a real life insurance pipeline.

Watch this first, then we can talk through what makes sense.

### Slide 1: The Problem

Here is the real problem I see with life insurance leads.

Most agents are paying for attention they do not fully control. A lead comes in, but that person may have filled out multiple forms, may not answer the first call, and may already be hearing from somebody else.

So the issue is not just whether you can buy more leads. The issue is what happens in the first few minutes after that lead shows interest.

If there is no fast response, no clean tracking, no follow-up path, and no way to see which leads are still alive, money leaks before you even get a fair shot.

That is why I look at this as a pipeline problem, not just a lead problem.

### Slide 2: What You Actually Need

What you need is ownership of the process after interest happens.

That means when somebody fills out a form, clicks an ad, or asks for information, the system should do a few things right away:

Capture the right information.

Confirm consent and route the lead properly.

Notify you fast.

Track the status.

Start follow-up if they do not book.

And move serious people toward your calendar.

That is the difference between buying leads and actually owning the pipeline.

### Slide 3: Phase 1 Overview

The first step is not building blind. The first step is the Lead Flow Audit.

I need to understand where your leads come from now, what happens after they come in, what tools you already use, which states you are licensed in, and what rules your carrier or upline has around marketing.

Once that is clear, Phase 1 becomes the first real build:

A focused landing page.

A life-insurance-specific intake form.

Consent language ready for approval.

Source tracking so you know where the lead came from.

A lead tracker.

Fast response.

Follow-up.

And calendar routing for people who are ready to talk.

The point is not to make it complicated. The point is to make sure every lead has a next step.

### Slide 4: Consent-Aware Follow-Up

One thing I want to be clear about: because this is life insurance, we do this the right way.

Before anything goes live, the public-facing language needs approval. That includes the landing page copy, SMS consent, opt-in and opt-out language, privacy language, and any carrier or upline restrictions.

I can build the system, write the first version, and structure the workflow. But you or your compliance contact need to approve the language before it goes public.

That protects you, and it protects the build.

### Slide 5: Paid Traffic Direction

For traffic, I would not go nationwide on day one.

That sounds exciting, but that is how people burn money before the system is proven.

I would start focused. Pick three to five states where you are licensed and where you actually want business. Test one or two lead sources separately, like Meta and Google, so we can see what is really creating conversations.

Then we look at the numbers:

Which state responded?

Which message worked?

Which leads booked?

Which source gave you people worth talking to?

Once the data points somewhere, then you scale. That is how you build a machine instead of just spending money on ads.

### Slide 6: What This Costs

For pricing, I would structure this in two lanes.

The focused pilot is $3,000 setup. That gives us one core workflow, one clean lead capture path, one tracker, and enough structure to prove whether this can work before we overbuild it.

After launch, monthly management is $1,350. That covers monitoring the workflow, fixing issues, adjusting follow-up, reviewing performance, and improving the pipeline as real leads come through.

If you want to go bigger from day one with multi-state routing, more advanced reporting, multiple lead sources, and more infrastructure, then we are closer to $4,500 to $5,000 setup with the same monthly management structure.

Ad spend is separate. That stays in your own Meta or Google account. I am not trying to hold your ad budget. My job is to build and manage the system that handles what happens after the lead comes in.

### Slide 7: What I Need From You

To move this forward, I need a few answers before I build anything serious.

Which states are you licensed in?

Which three to five states do you want to start with?

Where are your leads coming from right now?

What happens after somebody raises their hand?

Do you already have a booking link or calendar?

Do you have a CRM or are leads sitting in texts, email, or spreadsheets?

And who approves marketing language before it goes live?

Once I have those answers, I can turn this into a real implementation roadmap instead of guessing.
