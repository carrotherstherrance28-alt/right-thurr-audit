# Thurr Solutions LinkedIn-First B2B Content Plan

This plan turns Thurr Solutions into a visible operator brand before expanding into a community, YouTube cadence, or multi-platform content engine.

LinkedIn is the first channel because Thurr Solutions is B2B, service-led, and built around trust. The goal is to show owners that Thurr understands business leaks, follow-up problems, automation opportunities, and practical implementation.

## Position

Thurr Solutions builds private AI automation systems for local service businesses.

Primary audience:

- Contractors
- Salons and med spas
- Realtors and broker teams
- Funeral homes and professional services
- Mobile/local service operators
- Small agencies that need backend automation

What they care about:

- Missed leads
- Slow follow-up
- Manual quoting and intake
- No clear system for handoffs
- Inconsistent client communication
- No easy way to see what happened today
- Too many tools that do not talk to each other

Core promise:

```txt
We build. You profit.
```

Use that phrase as an operating promise, not a guaranteed financial claim. Pair it with practical language:

```txt
We build the intake, follow-up, reporting, and automation system so owners can stop losing opportunities in the gaps.
```

## Content Goals

- Build trust in Therrance as an operator and builder.
- Educate business owners on process leaks without overwhelming them.
- Show real systems being built: n8n, Supabase, Discord alerts, Thurnos drafts, Remotion content.
- Generate buildout plan requests, workflow audit conversations, and booked calls.
- Create proof assets for the website, proposals, and future case studies.

## Content Split

Use 80/20:

- 80% education, behind-the-scenes, proof, process, and founder/operator perspective.
- 20% direct offers, CTAs, diagnostics, and project availability.

Avoid turning every post into "book a call." The sales posts will work better after the educational posts show judgment.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Missed Lead Leaks

Purpose: make business owners recognize hidden revenue leaks.

Post angles:

- The lead responded, but nobody followed up.
- Why contact forms fail after submission.
- The difference between having a website and having a lead system.
- How fast follow-up changes owner workload.
- What a good intake form should collect.

Example hook:

```txt
Most businesses do not have a lead problem. They have a follow-up leak.
```

Post angles:

- Why "build a website" is not the first question.

- The 10 sections of an Autopilot Blueprint.
- Why strategy comes before automation.
- How to decide what to build first.

Example hook:

```txt
Before you automate the business, you have to know which system deserves to exist.
```

## Weekly Cadence

Start with 4 posts per week.

Recommended rhythm:

```txt
Monday    - Missed lead / business leak education
Tuesday   - Behind-the-scenes build proof
Thursday  - Local service automation example
Friday    - Founder/operator lesson or soft CTA
```

Commenting:

- Comment on 5-10 relevant posts per weekday.
- Comments should be 8+ words and add a real thought, not "great post."
- Prioritize local business owners, service operators, automation builders, and B2B creators.

## CTA Rules

Use one CTA per post.

Good CTAs:

- "Comment `buildout` and I will send the checklist."
- "DM me your current intake process and I will tell you where the leak is."
- "What part of your follow-up still happens manually?"
- "Which local service niche should I map next?"
- "Want me to turn this into a blueprint?"

Avoid:

- Multiple CTAs in one post.
- Big income claims.
- "Book a consultation" as the only ending.
- Generic AI hype.
- Making it sound like AI guarantees profit.

## What To Measure

Track weekly:

- Posts published.
- Comments made.
- Profile views.
- Connection requests sent/accepted.
- DMs started.
- Buildout plan requests.
- Workflow audit requests.
- Calls booked.
- Client/revenue outcomes later.

Tie this back to the analytics plan:

```txt
LinkedIn post -> profile/site click -> CTA click -> buildout form submitted -> blueprint generated -> booked-call intent
```

## Future Expansion

Only expand after 8-12 weeks of LinkedIn consistency:

- Repurpose strongest posts into short videos.
- Use Remotion for branded vertical clips.
- Publish YouTube walkthroughs only when there is a repeatable build/demo cadence.
- Start community planning only after people are consistently asking for support, templates, or shared sessions.
